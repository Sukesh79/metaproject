Social Impact

While metaverse could make you experience any environment, the disconnect from the real world could seriously impact our mental health. The same could be seen in a more aggressive form with a whole virtual world.

Privacy

As most of our personal and professional lives are spent online, our data has gradually become a commodity for many marketing companies. The advent of intrusive technologies has already raised our privacy concerns. Now the larger question is: how will metaverse control someone’s privacy.

Internet

For the Metaverse, we recommend a minimum of 1 Gbps (1000 Mbps) for both upstream and downstream traffic. If you are only using the Metaverse to view content, you may be able to get away with less bandwidth.

Requires technology

Not everyone may be able to afford the tech to experience metaverse since it does use pretty high-level equipment for an ultimate VR experience. The metaverse may directly impact and increase the socio-economic disparity.

psychological issues

1.Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder 2.Eating disorders 3.Anxiety, phobias and post-traumatic stress disorder 4.Autism 5.Alzheimer's disease 6.Stress and pain management

Eye Disability

Blind and visually impaired users will need screen reading capabilities along with seamless audio description. One challenge will be that the metaverse, by its very nature, is intended to be a visually richer world than what we have today. This will pose further complexities for blind and low vision users.

Virtual Crime

Most recently, the virtual worlds were in the news when a group of male virtual avatars molested a woman’s virtual avatar. This falls in a very grey area with the current criminal regulations. People often use social media to create fake identities as well. Even in the metaverse, this problem is bound to prevail.

Adverse impact on minors

It may be extremely difficult to keep children away from this futuristic tech. Therefore, it becomes imperative to protect the children from the harmful impact of the metaverse.
